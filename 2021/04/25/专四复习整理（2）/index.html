    解析

  23. 错选了C
    这句话就是what will be discussed …

  24. 错选C
    A强调句who/that均可，D是对的，we painted the kitchen white.

  25. 错选了A
    首先，assemble主动即可：to come together; gather; meet: e.g. We assembled in the auditorium.
    staff动词用复数或a member of staff，e.g. Although the blood-spattered offices will be off-limits, staff have vowed to continue producing the magazine.

  26. 错选了A
    alleviate: to make easier to endure; lessen; mitigate: e.g. to alleviate pain.
    diminish有及物动词的用法to make or cause to seem smaller,less important; reduce. e.g. They say the trial pause shouldn’t diminish hope in AstraZeneca’s vaccine. diminished responsibility是一个法律术语。
    devalue: to deprive of value e.g. Rosen undoubtedly did not intend to devalue women with her comments.

  27. 错选了B
    patch就是打补丁的意思: to mend, cover, or fill up a hole or weak spot in, e.g. The fence needs to be patched.
    还有一个很重要的意思to connect to a communication system，e.g. they patched him into the conference call

  28. C和D显然是错的，A也是对的，但B也不能说错
    stack exchange上有一个问题：‘I would say that anybody using it today is being deliberately archaic’

  29. 错选了B
    真会抠字眼

  30. 错选了B
    extend view似乎更适合抽象的观点

  31. 错选了C
    intricate:hard to understand, work, or make: e.g. an intricate machine.
    sophisticated(of a person, ideas, tastes, manners, etc.) altered by education, experience, etc., so as to be worldly-wise; not naive
    确实有sophisticated expertise的用法 e.g. Almost by definition, governing in the 21st century requires sophisticated expertise.

  32. 错选了A
    虚拟语气

  33. 错选了A
    解析说both不会发生转移否定

  34. 错选了B
    split hair: making unnecessary distinctions between things when the differences between them are so small they are not important. e.g. To argue about whether they arrived at two o’clock or at 2:01 is just splitting hairs

  35. 错选了A
    refined: free from coarseness, vulgarity,e.g. It feels refined, relaxed, upscale, and approachable all at the same time.
    elaborate:worked out with great care and nicety of detail e.g. elaborate preparations

  36. effective也不能说错
    emphatic:forceful; insistent, e.g. For the British grassroots initiative RunEqual, the answer to that question is an emphatic “yes.”

  37. 无聊的文字游戏

  38. 错选了C
    expanse就是这么用的： an uninterrupted space or area; e.g. an expanse of water.

  39. 解析说选his, whatever

  40. 错选了A
    commentary: a spoken description of an event on the radio or television that is broadcast as the event happens, e.g. The commentary on the Olympic Games was much better on the other channel.

  41. pace: to traverse or go over with steps, e.g. He paced the floor nervously.
    也有pace across的用法: We were watching Latham pace across a barge docked along the inland waterway.
    stroll: to walk leisurely, e.g. to stroll along the beach.
    stride:to take a long step, e.g. to stride across a puddle.
    swing基本没有走的意思
    解析说

    swung常与along/past/by连用，表示轻松地走。根据and前面的并列谓语jumped off可知杰克不可能悠闲地在草地上瞎逛，只能是大踏步走

    这个比较扯淡。硬要说的话jump和stride确实最搭，但这句话没有任何信息说A B不行。

  42. 错选了D
    be in the bag:If something is in the bag, you are certain to get it or to achieve it, e.g. Once we’d scored the third goal, the game was pretty much in the bag.
    注意这里是his election, 不是his campaign.

  43. 错选了A
    know by sight: to be familiar with the appearance of without having personal acquaintance, e.g. I know Mr Brown by sight but we have never spoken

  44. 错选了D
    assert: to say that something is certainly true, e.g. He asserts that she stole money from him.
    proclaim: to announce something publicly or officially, especially something positive, e.g. All the countries have proclaimed their loyalty to the alliance.

  45. 报刊课，美国世代的划分
    GI Gen从1900-1924年，参加二战。GI是galvanized iron，镀锌的铁，延申为美国大兵。
    silent gen/traditionalist从1925-1945，比较右
    baby boomer，1946-1964，战后婴儿潮
    Gen X从1965-1979，有人说X是十，因为美国1764年建国，到1965年刚好200年10代
    Gen Y/millennial 1980-1990
    Gen Z/Centennial 1990-2010，也叫snowflake，玻璃心的意思。
